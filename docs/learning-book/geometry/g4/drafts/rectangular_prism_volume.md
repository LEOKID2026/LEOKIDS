## 1. What are we learning?

Today in geometry we will learn to calculate the volume of a rectangular prism — a box, crate, or small storage space.
Volume = how much space is inside the solid — calculate: length × width × height.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

:::geometry-diagram
type: box_basic
:::

Rectangular prism — it has 3 dimensions:
- Length — front to back
- Width — right to left
- Height — bottom to top
Volume = length × width × height
Unit: cm³ (cubic centimeters).
Volume ≠ area — area is on a surface; volume is in space.

---

## 3. Example

Shoe box: length 3 cm, width 4 cm, height 5 cm.
- 3 × 4 = 12 (area of the bottom face)
- 12 × 5 = 60
Volume: 60 cm³ — 60 cubic units fit inside.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

A rectangular prism has length 3 cm, width 4 cm, and height 5 cm. What is the volume?
3 × 4 = 12

## 6. Let's check together

Rectangular prism: 2 × 4 × 6 cm — what is the volume?
Multiply the first two dimensions:
2 × 4 = 8
Multiply by the third dimension:
8 × 6 = 48
Volume = 48 cm³
