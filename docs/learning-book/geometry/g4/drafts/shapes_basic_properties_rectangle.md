## 1. What are we learning?

Today in geometry we will go deeper into the properties of a rectangle.
We will learn that a rectangle has 2 pairs of equal sides — not necessarily all four sides equal.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

:::geometry-diagram
type: rectangle_sides
:::

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles.
The key property here: 2 pairs of equal sides.
- One pair:
The long sides (opposite each other)
- Second pair:
The short sides (opposite each other)
Both sides in each pair are equal — but the long sides can be different from the short sides.

---

## 3. Example

A rectangle with sides 8 and 3:
- Two sides of length 8 (opposite)
- Two sides of length 3 (opposite)
2 pairs — not all 4 sides equal.
