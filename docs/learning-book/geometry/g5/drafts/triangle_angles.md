## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll explore why the sum of angles in a triangle is 180° — and use that to find a missing angle.
Idea: if you "cut out" all three angles and place them together — they form a straight line (180°).

---

## 2. Simple explanation

:::geometry-diagram
type: angle_basic
:::

In triangle ABC:
- angle A + angle B + angle C = 180°
Why? The three angles together "fill" the opening of a straight line — so the sum is always 180°.
If you know A and B — find C:
C = 180° − (A + B)

---

## 3. Example

In triangle ABC: A = 60°, B = 70°
Reasoning: 60° + 70° = 130° — "how much is left" until 180°?
180° − 130° = 50° → C = 50°
Check: 60° + 70° + 50° = 180° — the three angles "close" a straight line.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

In triangle ABC angle A = 60°, angle B = 70°. What is angle C?
Step 1 (reasoning): the two angles together = 60° + 70° = 130°.
Step 2 (missing): 180° − 130° = 50°
Step 3 (check): 60° + 70° + 50° = 180°
50°

## 5. Try it yourself

In triangle ABC: 45° and 95° — what is the third angle?
Try to solve by yourself.
On the next page we'll check the steps and the answer together.

## 6. Let's check together

In triangle ABC: angle A = 45°, angle B = 95° — what is angle C?
Add the two known angles:
45° + 95° = 140°
How much is left from 180°?
180° − 140° = 40°
Check: 45° + 95° + 40° = 180°
Angle C = 40°

## 7. Let's practice!

The 180° sum in a triangle is a rule with reasoning — not just a formula.
In practice you'll find a missing angle — add, subtract from 180°, and check that the sum is 180°!
