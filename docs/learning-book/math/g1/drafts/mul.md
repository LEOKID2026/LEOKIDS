## 1. What are we learning?

Today we're going to learn multiplication — and here we learn it in a simple way:
repeated addition of the same number, or equal groups.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

3 × 2 = three groups of 2
= 2 + 2 + 2 = 6
× means "groups of".
You can also say "times".
3 × 2 = 6 — three times two.
Here we use small multiplication: groups up to 5, and answers up to 20.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

3 × 2 — three groups of 2 stars:
Repeated addition: 2 + 2 + 2 = 6
Multiplication: 3 × 2 = 6
Example with stickers:
4 groups, 3 stickers in each group → 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12
→ 4 × 3 = 12

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

3 × 3 = ?
3 × 3 = three groups of 3.
Repeated addition: 3 + 3 + 3.
3 + 3 = 6, then 6 + 3 = 9.
3 × 3 = 9
