## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll find neighbors in big numbers — up to 10,000.
±1 even when crossing a thousand — for example 1,999 → 2,000 → 2,001.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Neighbor before = −1
Neighbor after = +1
2,450:
- before: 2,449
- after: 2,451
Crossing a thousand:
- 1,999 → after = 2,000
- 2,000 → before = 1,999
Place value: when you cross a thousand, all the digits can change — but the jump is still 1.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

3,628:
3,628 − 1 = 3,627
3,628 + 1 = 3,629
2,999 → 3,000: the neighbor after 2,999 = 3,000 — crossing a thousand, still just +1.
3,626 — 3,627 — [3,628] — 3,629 — 3,630

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

What is the neighbor before and after the number 3,999?
Before = 3,999 − 1 = 3,998.
After = 3,999 + 1 = 4,000 (crossing a thousand — still just +1).
Before 3,998, after 4,000
