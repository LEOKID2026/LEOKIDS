## 1. What are we learning?

Today we're going to learn that big numbers are built from hundreds, tens, and ones.
Think of bundles of 100, bundles of 10, and single cubes.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

A hundred = one group of 100 (a hundred chart / 100 cubes together).
A ten = one group of 10 (a ten-stick).
A one = a single cube.
For example, in the number 236:
- The digit 2 on the left = 2 hundreds (200)
- The digit 3 in the middle = 3 tens (30)
- The digit 6 on the right = 6 ones
200 + 30 + 6 = 236
Here we'll work a lot with numbers like 124, 236, and 405, and also numbers up to 1,000.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

Place value chart — the number 124:
What do you see?
- 1 hundred + 2 tens + 4 ones = 124
100 + 20 + 4 = 124
Another example — 405:
- 4 hundreds, 0 tens, 5 ones
400 + 0 + 5 = 405
Example with pencils:
1 bundle of 100 + 2 bundles of 10 + 4 single pencils = 124 pencils.

│ hundreds │ tens │ ones │
│ 1 │ 2 │ 4 │

= 124

## 4. Let's solve together

How many hundreds, tens, and ones are in 236?
Look at the digits: 2, 3, 6.
2 hundreds = 200, 3 tens = 30, 6 ones = 6
200 + 30 + 6 = 236
In 236 there are 2 hundreds, 3 tens, and 6 ones.

---

## 5. Try it yourself

How many hundreds, tens, and ones are in 312?
Try to solve it on your own.
On the next page we'll check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

How many hundreds, tens, and ones are in 312?
Step 1 — Digits: 3, 1, 2
3 hundreds = 300, 1 ten = 10, 2 ones = 2
300 + 10 + 2 = 312
In 312 there are 3 hundreds, 1 ten, and 2 ones.

---

## 7. Let's practice!

Now you know how to break a number into hundreds, tens, and ones.
In practice you'll find questions: how many hundreds?
How many tens?
How many ones?
Always start from the left digit!
