## 1. What are we learning?

Today we're going to learn word problems about coins.
When there are several coins of the same value, you can figure out how much money there is altogether — with addition or equal groups.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Each coin has a value — how many shekels it is worth.
Before you calculate, read:
1. What do we know? — how many coins, and the value of each
2. What are we asked? — how much money altogether
3. What do we do? — add, or multiply when there are equal groups
Equal groups: 4 coins of 5 ₪ → 4 × 5 = 20 (like in the times table).
Important: whole shekels only — no agorot. One step only.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

4 coins of 5 shekels:
4 × 5 = 20 → there are 20 shekels altogether.

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

There are 3 coins of 10 shekels.
How much money is there altogether?
Step 1 — What do we know? 3 coins, each worth 10 shekels.
Step 2 — What are we asked? How much money altogether.
Step 3 — What do we do? 3 × 10 = 30
There are 30 shekels altogether
