## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn about repeating decimals — digits that repeat forever.
1/3 = 0.333…

---

## 2. Simple explanation

When division does not end — sometimes you get a repeating pattern.
1 ÷ 3 = 0.333333… — the digit 3 repeats.
Write: 0.3̄ (a bar over 3) or 0.333…

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

2/3 = 0.666… — the repeating digit is 6.
1/6 = 0.1666… — the repeating digit is 6.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

What is 1/3 as a decimal?
1 ÷ 3 = 0.333…
0.333… (repeating 3).

---

## 5. Try it yourself

Write 2/3 as a decimal number.
Try to solve it on your own.
On the next page we will check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Write 2/3 as a decimal number
2 ÷ 3 = ?
3 goes into 20 → 6 times (18), remainder 2; again 3 goes into 20 → 6… → repeating.
2 ÷ 3 = 0.666… — the repeating digit is 6.
The answer: 0.666… (repeating 6)

---

## 7. Let's practice!

Repeating decimals connect fractions to decimals.
In practice you will find fractions that do not end — look for the repeating pattern!
