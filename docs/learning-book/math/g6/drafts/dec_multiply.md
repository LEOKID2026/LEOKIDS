## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn to multiply decimals.
1.5 × 2.4 — multiply as whole numbers, then count digits after the decimal point.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

1.5 × 2.4:
15 × 24 = 360
One digit + one digit = 2 digits after the decimal point
360 → 3.60

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

0.3 × 0.2 = 0.06 — two digits after the decimal point.

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Calculate: 2.3 × 1.6
Multiply as whole numbers: 23 × 16 = 368
Count digits after the decimal point: 1 + 1 = 2 digits.
368 → 3.68
Check: 3.68 ÷ 2.3 = 1.6
The answer: 3.68
