## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn to discover the rule of a sequence — and then fill in missing terms or continue forward.
We work with numbers up to 200,000, including sequences that go up and down.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Step 1 — find the rule: calculate the difference between two consecutive numbers.
500, 550, 600, …
- 550 − 500 = 50
- 600 − 550 = 50
- The rule: a step of +50
Step 2 — use the rule: add (or subtract) the same step to find a missing term or the next one.
Big example: 125,000, 125,500, 126,000 — the same rule +500.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

A decreasing sequence: 180,000, 179,950, 179,900, ?, ?
179,950 − 180,000 = −50
179,900 − 179,950 = −50
The rule: −50 at each step
179,900 − 50 = 179,850
179,850 − 50 = 179,800
The answer: 179,850, 179,800

---

## 4. Let's solve together

Continue: 500, 550, 600, ? , ?
550 − 500 = 50 → the rule is +50.
600 + 50 = 650; 650 + 50 = 700
650, 700

## 5. Try it yourself

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Continue: 3,000, 3,250, 3,500, ?, ?
Step 1 — find the rule: 3,250 − 3,000 = 250; 3,500 − 3,250 = 250 → the rule: +250.
3,500 + 250 = 3,750
3,750 + 250 = 4,000
The answer: 3,750, 4,000
