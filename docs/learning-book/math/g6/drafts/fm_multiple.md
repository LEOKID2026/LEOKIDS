## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will strengthen multiples — results of n × 1, n × 2, n × 3, …
Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, 32, …

---

## 2. Simple explanation

8 × 1 = 8
8 × 2 = 16
8 × 3 = 24
8 × 4 = 32
Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, …

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

5 × 7 = 35 — 35 is a multiple of 5 and also of 7.

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

First 5 multiples of 6
6 × 1 = 6
6 × 2 = 12
6 × 3 = 18
6 × 4 = 24
6 × 5 = 30
The answer: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30
