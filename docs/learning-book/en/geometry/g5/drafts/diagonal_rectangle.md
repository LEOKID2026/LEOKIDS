## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll learn the diagonal of a rectangle.
The diagonal is the hypotenuse in a right triangle — with length and width as the legs.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

:::geometry-diagram
type: rectangle_diagonal
:::

Formula:
Diagonal = √(length² + width²)
(Connected to the idea of the Pythagorean theorem — we'll go deeper later)

---

## 3. Example

Rectangle 6 cm × 8 cm:
√(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 cm

---

## 4. Let's solve together

Rectangle 6 cm by 8 cm. Diagonal?
6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100
√100 = 10
10 cm.

---

## 5. Try it yourself

Rectangle 5 cm × 12 cm — what is the length of the diagonal?
Try to solve by yourself.
On the next page we'll check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Let's check together

Rectangle 5 cm × 12 cm — what is the length of the diagonal?
Square the legs:
5² = 25
12² = 144
Add:
25 + 144 = 169
Square root:
√169 = 13
Diagonal = 13 cm

---

## 7. Let's practice!

Diagonal of a rectangle = √(length² + width²).
In practice you'll find diagonals — first square each leg!
