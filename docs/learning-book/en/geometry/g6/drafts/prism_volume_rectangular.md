## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn volume of a prism — a solid with two identical bases (rectangle) and rectangular faces.
Formula: volume = base area × height.
First calculate the area of the base — then "stretch" it by the height.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

:::geometry-diagram
type: box_basic
:::

Base area = length × width
Volume = base area × height
Height — the perpendicular distance between the two bases.

---

## 3. Example

Prism: base 6 cm × 4 cm, height 10 cm:
- Base area: 6 × 4 = 24 cm²
- Volume: 24 × 10 = 240 cm³
"24 square units in the base, 10 layers" → 240 unit cubes.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

Prism — base 6 cm × 4 cm, height 10 cm. What is the volume?
Base area — 6 × 4 = 24.
Volume — 24 × 10 = 240.
240 cm³.
