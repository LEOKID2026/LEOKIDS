---

## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll learn what a power means — short writing for repeated multiplication of the same number.
10³ = 10 to the power of 3.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

10³ — read: "ten to the power of three" (or "10 cubed").
10 = the base — the number that repeats
3 = the exponent — how many times you multiply
10³ = 10 × 10 × 10
10 × 10 = 100
100 × 10 = 1,000
10³ = 1,000
Rule: exponent 3 → multiply the base 3 times.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

5² — base 5, exponent 2.
5² = 5 × 5 = 25
2 in the exponent → two factors of 5.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

What does 2⁴ mean? (Base? Exponent? Write it as repeated multiplication.)
Base = 2, exponent = 4.
2⁴ = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 — four factors of 2.
2⁴ = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

What does 5³ mean?
Write it as repeated multiplication — and what's the value?
Base = 5, exponent = 3 — three factors of 5.
5³ = 5 × 5 × 5
5 × 5 = 25, then 25 × 5 = 125.
5³ = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125
