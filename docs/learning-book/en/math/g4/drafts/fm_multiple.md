## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll learn about multiples of a number.
A multiple = the result of multiplying the number by 1, 2, 3, 4…

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Multiples of 5:
5 × 1 = 5
5 × 2 = 10
5 × 3 = 15
5 × 4 = 20
5 × 5 = 25
5 × 6 = 30 …
Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30…
Link to factors: if 5 is a factor of 20 — then 20 is a multiple of 5.
Tip: multiples of 5 end in 0 or 5 (like divisibility rules).

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

Multiples of 4 (the first ones):
4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32…
4 × 1, 4 × 2, 4 × 3, 4 × 4, 4 × 5…

## 4. Let's solve together

Write the first 5 multiples of 7.

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Write the first 5 multiples of 6.
6 × 1 = 6
6 × 2 = 12
6 × 3 = 18
6 × 4 = 24
6 × 5 = 30
6, 12, 18, 24, 30
