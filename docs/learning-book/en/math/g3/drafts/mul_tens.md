## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn to multiply by tens — for example 4 × 30.
It's like multiplying by 4, then adding a 0 (moving to the tens place).

---

## 2. Simple explanation

4 × 30:
Think: 4 × 3 = 12
Add a 0 (because 30 = 3 tens): 120
4 × 30 = 120
Rule: multiply the digit (without the 0), then put a 0 at the end.
6 × 5 = 30, so 6 × 50 = 300

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Calculate: 8 × 60
8 × 6 = 48
Add a 0 (because 60 = 6 tens): 480
8 × 60 = 480
