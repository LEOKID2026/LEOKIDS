## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn to read numbers up to 1,000 and figure out what each digit means by its place.
In a big number there are hundreds, tens, and ones.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Each digit in a number sits in its own place:
The hundreds digit — how many hundreds (100, 200, 300…)
The tens digit — how many tens (10, 20, 30…)
The ones digit — how many single units (1, 2, 3…)
472 = 4 hundreds + 7 tens + 2 ones = 400 + 70 + 2
Important: The 4 in 472 is hundreds (400), not 4 ones.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

638 — breaking it apart by place:
600 + 30 + 8 = 638
6 in the hundreds place → 600
3 in the tens place → 30
8 in the ones place → 8
638 = six hundred thirty-eight.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

In the number 527 — what is the value of the hundreds digit?
Find the digits: 5 | 2 | 7
The hundreds digit is 5.
5 in the hundreds place = 500.
The value of the hundreds digit in 527 is 500.

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

In the number 836 — what is the value of the hundreds digit?
Find the digits: 8 | 3 | 6
The hundreds digit is 8 — it sits in the first place from the left.
8 in the hundreds place = 800
836 = 800 + 30 + 6

## 7. Let's practice!

Now you know how to break a number up to 1,000 into hundreds, tens, and ones.
In practice you'll find questions about a digit's value by its place.
Always check the place!
