## 1. What are we learning?

Today we're going to learn to count backward on the number line.
It's like a countdown before a show — 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, 0!

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Counting backward is like walking to the left on the number line.
Each step to the left = one number smaller.
For example: 8, 7, 6, 5 — each number is one less than the one before it.
Important: In this chapter, when you reach 0 — you stop and don't go below 0.

---

## 4. Let's solve together

Count backward from 7. What are the next three numbers?
Put your finger on 7 on the number line.
Hop one step to the left → 6
Another step to the left → 5
Another step to the left → 4
The next three numbers are 6, 5, 4.
If you write the whole sequence from the start: 7, 6, 5, 4.

---

## 5. Try it yourself

Count backward from 9.
What are the next two numbers?
Try to solve it on your own.
On the next page we'll check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Count backward from 9.
What are the next two numbers?
Put your finger on 9 on the number line.
Hop one step to the left → 8.
Another step to the left → 7.
The next two numbers are 8, 7.
