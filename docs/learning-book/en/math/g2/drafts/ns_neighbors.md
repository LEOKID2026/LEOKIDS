## 1. What are we learning?

Today we're going to learn to find neighbors even when we cross ten — for example, from 199 to 200.
A neighbor is always ±1, even when the tens or hundreds change.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Neighbor before = the number minus 1.
Neighbor after = the number plus 1.
Crossing ten — an important example:
- 199 → neighbor after = 200 (not 190!)
- 200 → neighbor before = 199 (not 100!)
248:
- Before: 247
- After: 249
The rule: +1 or −1 — not a jump of 10.

What are the neighbors of 248?

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

What are the neighbors of 199?
Step 1 — Neighbor before: 199 − 1 = 198
Step 2 — Neighbor after: 199 + 1 = 200 (crossing ten — we reach 200!)
The neighbors of 199 are 198 and 200
