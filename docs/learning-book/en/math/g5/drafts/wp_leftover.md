## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll solve a word problem — how many are left?
When you fill equal boxes (or groups), the answer is the remainder — not the number of boxes!

---

## 2. Simple explanation

A crate of apples: 127 apples, 15 in each box.
Step 1 — division: 127 ÷ 15 = 8 full boxes and remainder 7.
Step 2 — meaning: 8 = how many full boxes; 7 = how many apples are left (remainder).
Step 3 — check: 8 × 15 + 7 = 127
"How many are left?" → 7 (the remainder), not 8.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

340 oranges, 12 in each basket.
340 ÷ 12 = 28 full baskets and remainder 4.
4 oranges are left — that's the remainder.
Check: 28 × 12 + 4 = 340

---

## 4. Let's solve together

127 apples, 15 in each box. How many apples are left after filling the boxes?
127 ÷ 15 = 8 remainder 7.
8 × 15 = 120; 127 − 120 = 7 (remainder).
7 apples are left (remainder).

---

## 5. Try it yourself

185 eggs, 12 in each carton.
How many eggs are left after filling the cartons?
Try to solve it on your own.
On the next page we'll check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

185 eggs, 12 in each carton.
How many eggs are left?
185 ÷ 12 = 15 full cartons and remainder 5
15 × 12 = 180; 185 − 180 = 5 (remainder)
Step 3 — check: 15 × 12 + 5 = 180 + 5 = 185
5 eggs are left

---

## 7. Let's practice!

In a word problem.

Look for the remainder!
