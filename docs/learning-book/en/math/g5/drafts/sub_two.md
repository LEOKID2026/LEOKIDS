## 1. What are we learning?

Today we'll learn to subtract a large number from a large number — up to 100,000.
We'll break apart by place value and borrow when we need to.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Steps for subtraction:
Break apart by place value
Subtract the ones — if there aren't enough, borrow 1 from the tens
Subtract the tens — borrow from the hundreds
Continue with hundreds, thousands, ten-thousands
50,000 − 12,375:
You need to borrow in many places — work step by step.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

38,400 − 9,256:
Ones: 0 < 6 → borrow → 10−6=4
Tens: 9 (after borrowing) − 5 = 4
Hundreds: 3 − 2 = 1
Thousands: 7 (after borrowing) − 9 — borrow → 17−9=8
Ten-thousands: 2 (after borrowing) − 0 = 2
38,400 − 9,256 = 29,144

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Work out: 70,000 − 23,456 = ?
Ones: 0 − 6 — borrow → 10 − 6 = 4
Tens: 9 (after borrowing) − 5 = 4
Hundreds: 9 (after borrowing) − 4 = 5
Thousands: 9 (after borrowing) − 3 = 6
Ten-thousands: 6 (after borrowing) − 2 = 4
70,000 − 23,456 = 46,544
