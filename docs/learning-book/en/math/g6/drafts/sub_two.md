## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn subtraction up to 200,000.
When a digit is too small — borrow from the column to the left.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

50,200 − 18,475:
- ones: 0<5 → borrow → 10−5=5
- tens: 9−7=2 (after borrowing)
- hundreds: 1<4 → borrow…
- …
= 31,725

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Calculate: 73,400 − 29,365
Ones: 0 < 5 → borrow. 10 − 5 = 5.
Tens: after borrowing there are 9 tens. 9 − 6 = 3.
Hundreds: after borrowing there are 3 hundreds. 3 − 3 = 0.
Thousands: 3 < 9 → borrow from ten thousands. 13 − 9 = 4.
Ten thousands: after borrowing there are 6 ten thousands. 6 − 2 = 4.
Check: 44,035 + 29,365 = 73,400
The answer: 44,035
