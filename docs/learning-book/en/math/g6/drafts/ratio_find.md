## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will learn to find a missing quantity when the ratio is known.
Ratio 2:5 — total 35.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

2:5 → 2+5=7 parts.
35 ÷ 7 = 5 per part.
Part 2: 2×5=10
Part 5: 5×5=25

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

3:4, total 28 → 7 parts, 28÷7=7 → 21 and 7

---

## 4. Let's solve together

Ratio 2:5, total 35. What are the two parts?
2+5=7, 35÷7=5
2×5=10, 5×5=25
10 and 25.

---

## 5. Try it yourself

Ratio 1:3, total 40 — what are the two parts?

---

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

Ratio 1:3, total 40
Number of parts: 1+3=4
Value of each part: 40÷4=10
Part 1: 1×10=10; Part 3: 3×10=30.
Check: 10+30=40
The answer: 10 and 30
