## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will strengthen pairs that make 100 — and see how they connect to decimals and percents.
When two parts together make 100% (or 1 whole) — it is the same idea as 63 + 37 = 100.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

63 + 37 = 100 — a familiar pair.
Connection to decimals:
- 0.63 + 0.37 = 1.00 (one whole)
- 0.63 = 63 hundredths → 63%
- 0.37 = 37 hundredths → 37%
- 63% + 37% = 100%
More pairs:
- 82 + 18 = 100 → 0.82 + 0.18 = 1.00
- 45 + 55 = 100 → 45% + 55% = 100%
The idea: two parts that complete a whole — whether 100, 1.00, or 100%.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

18 + ? = 100
100 − 18 = 82
Check: 18 + 82 = 100
Connection: 18% + 82% = 100%
0.18 + 0.82 = 1.00

---

## 4. Let's solve together

63 + ? = 100 — what is the missing number?
100 − 63 = 37
63 + 37 = 100
Step 3 (connection): 63% + 37% = 100%
The missing number is 37.

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

45 + ? = 100
? = 100 − 45 = 55
Check: 45 + 55 = 100
Step 3 (connection): 45% + 55% = 100%
The answer: 55
