## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will solve: what's left? (the remainder)
850 packages, 6 in each box — how many are left after filling full boxes?

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Warehouse: 850 packages, 6 in each box — how many are left after filling full boxes?
850 ÷ 6 = 141 full boxes and remainder 4.
Step 2 — check: 141 × 6 + 4 = 846 + 4 = 850
141 = full boxes; 4 = packages left over (remainder).
"How many are left?" → 4, not 141.

---

## 3. Visual / concrete example

612 bottles, 8 on each tray.
612 ÷ 8 = 76 full trays and remainder 4.
4 bottles were left over (remainder).
Check: 76 × 8 + 4 = 612

---

## 4. Let's solve together

850 packages, 6 per box. How many packages are left?
850 ÷ 6 = 141 with remainder 4.
141 × 6 = 846; 850 − 846 = 4 (remainder).
4 packages were left over (remainder).

## 6. Common mistake — watch out!

730 packages, 9 per box — how many are left?
730 ÷ 9 = 81 full boxes and remainder 1.
81 × 9 = 729; remainder: 730 − 729 = 1.
Step 3 — check: 81 × 9 + 1 = 729 + 1 = 730
The answer: 1 package left

## 7. Let's practice!
