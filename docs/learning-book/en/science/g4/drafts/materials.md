## 1. What are we learning?

Today in science we will learn about changes in materials, and basic electricity concepts: conductor and insulator.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Physical change — the material stays the same material, only the shape changes.
Example: ice melts to water — still water.
Chemical change — a new material is formed.
Example: a baked egg — does not return to a liquid egg.
Electricity (concepts only):
- Conductor — a material that allows electricity to pass through (metal like iron, copper)
- Insulator — a material that makes it hard for electricity to pass (plastic, rubber)
A metal coin — conductor:
Electricity can pass through it.
A plastic pen — insulator:
Electricity almost does not pass through it.
There are no instructions for wiring circuits here — only understanding.

## 6. Let's check together

The question: a rubber balloon, a metal spoon, a wool string — which conducts electricity? Which is an insulator?
Scientific explanation:
A conductor is a material that allows electricity to flow through it easily — usually metals.
An insulator is a material that makes it very hard for electricity to pass — like rubber, plastic, wool, wood.
A metal spoon is made of metal (like stainless steel) — a good conductor.
A rubber balloon is made of rubber — an excellent natural insulator.
A wool string is made of natural fibers — also an insulator; electricity does not pass easily.
Metal spoon — conductor. Rubber balloon — insulator. Wool string — insulator.
