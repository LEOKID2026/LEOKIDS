## 1. What are we learning?

Today we will summarize what we learned in science about plants — the last lesson summary on this topic.
We will connect plant parts, growing conditions, and the role of light.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Remember:
- Root — absorbs water and holds the plant in the ground
- Stem — holds up the plant
- Leaf — green, absorbs light
A plant needs sun, water, and suitable soil.
An important process:
Inside the leaves, with the help of sunlight, the plant makes food for itself.
We call this making food: it is not food like humans eat, but the idea is similar.
Without enough light — the leaves start to pale and the plant weakens.
Imagine a leaf like a small factory:
Input — light, water, and air; output — food for the plant.

---

## 3. Example

Two identical pots in the classroom — both on the same sunny window.
Pot A gets the right amount of water.
Pot B gets almost no water.
After two weeks:
- A — green leaves, strong plant
- B — wilted leaves, weak plant
The difference:
Amount of water — even with light, without water the plant cannot make food properly.

## 5. Try it yourself

Imagine a plant that grows for two weeks in a room with no window — no sunlight at all.
What will happen to its leaves?
What will happen to food production in the plant?
Which growing condition is missing?
Try to solve it on your own.
On the next page we will check the steps and the answer together.

---

## 6. Let's check together

A plant without sunlight cannot make food inside its leaves.
Without food production — the plant weakens, the leaves lose their green color and turn pale.
Light is a necessary condition for making food; without it the plant cannot function.
The leaves will turn pale, the plant will weaken — the missing growing condition is sunlight.
Light, water, and suitable soil are important growing conditions; without light there is no food production in leaves.
