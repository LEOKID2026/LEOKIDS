## 1. What are we learning?

Today in science we will learn about the human body — how we feel the world and move around.
We have senses that help us see, hear, smell, taste, and touch.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Our body has five ways to know the world — the five senses:
Eyes — we see colors and shapes
Ears — we hear sounds
Nose — we smell scents
Tongue — we taste sweet, salty, and more
Skin — we feel hot, cold, soft, or hard
We also move:
Legs help us walk, hands help us hold things and write.
In our chest we have a heart.
The heart is a muscle that works all the time and pumps blood through the whole body.

---

## 3. Example

Danny wants to know what is happening outside.
He walks to the window and looks — he uses his eyes to see.
Then Mom calls him — he hears the voice in his ears.
Danny sees, hears, and moves — that is how the senses and the body work together.

## 4. Let's solve together

Which body part do we use to see birds in the sky?
To see — that is connected to the sense of sight.
The body part for sight is the eyes.

## 6. Let's check together

Which sense do we use to smell a flower?
When we smell a flower — our nose picks up tiny scent particles that come from it.
The nose sends a message to the brain, and the brain tells us if the smell is pleasant or sharp.
Every flower smells a little different — that is the sense of smell helping us know.
The nose — it is the body part that helps us smell flowers and other things.
