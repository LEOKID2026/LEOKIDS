## 1. What are we learning?

Today in science we will learn about basic chemistry — changes in materials, density — and safety in all work with materials.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Chemistry — the study of materials and changes:
- Reversible change — you can go back, for example ice that becomes water and returns to ice
- Irreversible change — does not go back (a baked egg)
Density — how much material or mass is in a certain volume:
- A material with density higher than water — sinks (coin in water)
- A material with density lower than water — floats (wood)
Safety (important!):
- Do not taste or smell unknown materials
- Work with a teacher only
- Protect eyes and hands
- No home experiments with cleaning products or medicines

---

## 3. Example

A coin in a cup of water — sinks (high density).
A piece of wood in a cup of water — floats (low density).
An egg in a pan — irreversible change.

## 6. Let's check together

The question: folding paper versus burning paper — which change is reversible, and which is not? And which safety rule is important?
Scientific explanation:
A reversible change — you can return to the original material; no new material is formed.
Folding paper — the paper changed shape; you can unfold it — reversible change.
Burning paper — ash, heat, and smoke are formed; the paper no longer exists — irreversible change.
Safety:
Fire is dangerous, so students do not do fire experiments at home or alone. If there is a demonstration in class, only a responsible teacher does it; if there is danger, move away and call an adult immediately.
Folding — reversible change. Burning — irreversible change. Safety — do not try alone.
