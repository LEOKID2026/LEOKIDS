## 1. What are we learning?

Today in science we will learn about weather, climate, and the water cycle on Earth.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Weather — what happens in the sky today or this week:
Sun, rain, wind, heat.
Climate — the average weather over a long time (months and years) in an area.
The water cycle — water moves between the sea, sky, and land:
In the first stage, the sun heats water in the sea and some of it turns into vapor.
In the second stage, the vapor rises, cools, and condenses into tiny drops in clouds.
In the third stage, rain falls and the water returns to the sea and land.
In the fourth stage, plants, animals, and people use water — and the cycle continues.
Imagine a circle:
Water from the sea evaporates, forms clouds, falls as rain, flows in streams, and returns to the sea.

---

## 3. Example

On a rainy winter day — cold and wet weather.
In Israel summers are usually hot and dry, and winters bring rain — a pattern like this over years is climate.
Rain falling today is weather.
A hot summer that returns here every year is part of the climate.

## 6. Let's check together

On a sunny day, the sun heats the puddle water.
The water evaporates — goes from liquid to vapor that rises into the air.
The vapor cools and condenses into tiny drops in clouds — and so the water cycle continues.
The puddle water evaporates and rises into the air — that is the evaporation stage in the water cycle.
Evaporation — liquid water turns into vapor; then clouds form and rain falls.
