## 1. What are we learning?

Today in science we will learn about Earth's layers, resources beneath the surface — and phenomena like earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.

---

## 2. Simple explanation

Earth is built in layers.
We live on the crust — the outer thin layer of Earth.
Below the crust are warmer areas and rocks that move very slowly over time.
An earthquake — a sudden movement of rocks or plates in Earth's crust, releasing energy and causing shaking felt on the surface.
A volcano — a place where hot material from inside Earth can reach the surface.
Resources like groundwater and rocks are also found within the crust.

---

## 3. Example

In Israel — mostly stable; sometimes light earthquakes.
In places with active volcanoes — there are warning systems and safety plans.
Groundwater resources — important for farming; need to be protected and not pumped too much.

## 6. Let's check together

The question: water from a well — where does it come from? And which natural phenomenon is related to movement inside Earth?
Scientific explanation:
Groundwater is found within the crust — in rock and sand layers that hold water in spaces between them.
A well is dug to reach those layers and pump water for use.
An earthquake happens when rocks or plates in Earth's crust move suddenly and release energy — and the shaking reaches the surface.
Scientists measure and track with special instruments.
Water from a well — comes from the crust (groundwater). Phenomenon — earthquake (movement inside Earth).
